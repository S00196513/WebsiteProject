How to Make Dried Mushrooms

The process of drying mushrooms intensifies individual flavours and scents, meaning each species will have more exaggerated chrarcteristics. Drying mushrooms is great for preserving them for months at a time too and is relatively easy to learn how to do so why not give it a shot

First wash the mushrooms gently with a damp cloth but don't rinse them as this will damage their delicate tissue. Also check there are no infestations of insects and remove large stalks.

Then cut the mushrooms into thin slics and place onto a baking sheet lined with several layers of newspaper. Leave in a well ventilated room at 20degress celsius for 2 days. Alternatively, place on a baling tray and put in the oven at 60 degrees celsius for 2 hours if you want a faster method.

Once the mushrooms aree fully dry transfer them to an air tight jar and store in a cool, dry place. If the mushrooms aren't completely dry, mold can grow on them so be careful. It's as easy as that!

Saffron Mushrooms with Parsley, Butter and Pine Nuts on Toast

Ingredients
  • Pine Nuts 25grams
  • Vegetable Oil 1 Tablespoon
  • Butter 50 grams
  • Shallot (1)
  • Mushrooms 350grams
  • Double Cream 45ml
  • Fresh Parsley 75ml
  • Pinch of salt and Pepper
  • 4 Slices Crusty Brown Bread
  • Saffron 10ml
Method
  1. Saute the pine nuts over a moderate heat until browned.
  2. In a new pan cook the chopped shallots in half the butter on low heat until soft. Add the mushrooms and remaining buter until they're soft too.Stir in the double cream and cook until thick.Stir in Parsley and saffron and add salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Toast the bread and spread the mushrooms over and spinkle the pine nuts over for added crucnch. Enjoy!
